7. Analysis & Recommendation

What the data tells us so far

1. Messaging alone doesn't meaningfully lift franchise completion. All three cohorts show nearly identical completion rates (~5 per 1,000 over 15 days). The stick messages haven't materially changed customer behavior toward filing.

2. Messaging does drive marginal FS re-engagement. Unassign messaging shows a slight lift in FS actions (14.6/1K vs 12.7/1K holdout over 15 days). The effect is most pronounced in the first 3 days post-message. This suggests the messages do prompt some customers to take action — just not enough to reach completion.

3. Unassign preserves the FS path while clearing expert assignment. Post-unassign, customers continue to take FS actions (4.6/1K within 5 days). The engagement stays active — if a customer re-engages, they can get a new expert. This means unassign clears expert time without permanently closing the door.

4. Detach impact is still premature to judge. The 3/24 re-run just happened. Only 5 franchise completions post-action vs unassign's 41 — but unassign has had 5x more days. The critical unknown: will detached customers re-engage via DIWM/DIY? Zero have so far, but it's only been 1 day.

5. No DIWM/DIY re-engagement detected in any cohort. This is the biggest signal for the detach vs. unassign decision. If detach is supposed to redirect customers to DIWM/DIY, there's zero evidence of that pathway activating yet.

Directional Recommendation

Lean into Unassign for the rest of the season.

The data supports unassign as the lower-risk path that achieves the operational goal (clearing WIP / protecting expert time) without permanently losing the customer from FS. Key reasons:

  • FS path stays open: Unassigned customers continue taking FS actions (4.6/1K within 5 days post-action). Some will complete on their own.
  • No evidence of DIWM/DIY re-engagement: Detach's theoretical upside (redirecting to DIWM) hasn't materialized. Zero post-message DIWM starts across all cohorts.
  • Operationally simpler: Unassign executed cleanly on 3/19. Detach required 2 attempts and hit technical issues (duplicates, circuit breaker).
  • Reversible: Unassigned customers can be re-assigned if they re-engage. Detached customers need to start over.

However: Re-read in 5–7 days (by 3/30) to check if detached customers show any DIWM/DIY re-engagement. If they do, the calculus changes — detach could unlock incremental revenue from DIWM completions that wouldn't happen under unassign.
