9. Statistical Interpretation & Recommendation

What the statistics tell us

1. Against the full holdout, treatments show no significant lift in FS filing. Both detach (ITC=67, p=0.103) and unassign (ITC=71, p=0.150) produce nearly identical FS filing rates to the holdout.

2. Against the low-intent holdout, a real signal emerges. The low-intent holdout had 0 FS filing(s), while both treatments produced ~0.6% filing (p < 0.001). This suggests the messaging + intervention activated some behavior in what would have been fully dormant customers. However, the absolute lift is small.

3. Detach is the only lever that significantly clears WIP. Detach's 40% off-queue rate is significantly higher than holdout (5%, p < 0.001 ***). Unassign's off-queue rate (4%) is significantly different from holdout.

4. Unassign preserves optionality without clearing WIP. Unassign shows 69 post-action filings in 7 days (4/1K) vs detach's 22 (1/1K). On a per-day basis, these are comparable. But unassigned engagements remain 'WIP' with expert removed — this doesn't actually free up the engagement slot.

5. Zero DIWM/DIY re-engagement across all cohorts. Detach's theoretical advantage — redirecting customers to self-file — has produced zero conversions. No new DIWM/DIY starts post-message in any group.

Net Assessment

For WIP clearing: Detach wins. It's the only action that significantly moves engagements off expert queues (ITC=745, p < 0.001 ***). If the primary objective is protecting expert capacity, detach is the statistically validated lever.

For customer outcomes: No significant difference. Neither treatment meaningfully improves FS filing or franchise completion vs holdout.

Detach's DIWM upside remains unproven. Zero post-message DIWM starts is a strong null signal.

Recommendation: Detach for immediate WIP clearing if that's the operational priority. Re-read at day 7 post-detach (by 3/31) to check for DIWM emergence. If none, detach's only advantage over unassign is the system-closure, with no customer outcome benefit.
