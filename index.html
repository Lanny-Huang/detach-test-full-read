Analysis & Recommendations

Key Finding 1: Messaging alone drove the dominant effect. Both treatment cohorts show nearly identical FS filing rates (~0.5%) and re-engagement patterns compared to holdout (0.53%). The messaging itself — not the actual detach/unassign action — appears to be the primary driver of any behavior change observed so far.
Key Finding 2: Detach created more "closed" engagements. The detach cohort has 5.31% completed-but-not-filed engagements vs. 2.73% for unassign and 2.69% for holdout. This 2.6pp delta reflects the detach process closing the FS engagement. These customers' FS engagements were revoked, which means their engagement was completed (closed) without filing — not that they abandoned on their own.
Key Finding 3: Too early for definitive conclusions on detach vs unassign. Detach was only executed last night (3/24). The DIWM re-engagement, franchise completion, and revenue data will take days to materialize. Recommend re-reading this test in 3-5 days to see if detached customers re-engage in DIWM/DIY and at what rate.
Key Finding 4: ~80% of all cohorts remain at Welcome Call with no progress. This confirms the original low-intent classification was accurate. These engagements are consuming expert capacity without customer action.

Preliminary Directional Perspective

For rest of season WIP clearing: Both paths are viable for clearing WIP to protect expert time. The unassign path is operationally simpler (fewer system failures, no circuit breaker issues) and frees expert capacity while keeping the customer in FS — preserving the option for them to return. Detach is a harder action (moves customer out of FS entirely) but may drive franchise completion via DIWM if customers re-engage on their own.

Recommended next read: 3/28-3/30 for mature detach outcomes. Key metrics to watch: (1) DIWM re-engagement rate for detached customers, (2) franchise completion rate across all 3 cohorts, (3) revenue per customer. If detach customers show meaningful DIWM completion, that favors the detach path. If not, unassign is the lower-risk path with equivalent WIP clearing.
